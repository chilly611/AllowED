Frequently Asked Questions

What is VA certification? ▼

VA certification is the process where your school (via the Student Certifying Official, or SCO) confirms your enrollment to the Department of Veterans Affairs. This certification authorizes your VA education benefits to be paid. Each semester, your SCO must certify your enrollment in courses to activate your benefits.

How long does certification take? ▼

Typically, certification moves through all 5 stages in 3-5 business days. The time depends on course verification (especially non-standard courses) and VA processing volume. We'll show you how long each stage is taking on this page.

Why is my certification delayed? ▼

Delays usually happen when: (1) your schedule includes courses the SCO needs to verify (e.g., independent studies, cross-listed courses), or (2) the VA is processing a high volume of certifications. Check your status page for specific details. If stuck for more than 7 days, contact your campus veterans center.

What do I do if my status is "In Queue" but action is needed? ▼

If you see an orange alert with action needed, your SCO may contact you about courses that need verification. Don't wait—reach out to your SCO directly to confirm your course information. This speeds up the certification process.

Where do I find my Red ID? ▼

Your Red ID is a 9-digit number on your student ID card, in your email address (usually the first part before @), or in the campus student portal under "My Account."

How often is this page updated? ▼

Your certification status updates automatically as soon as the SCO or VA system changes it. No need to refresh manually—we'll notify you when your status changes. Check the "Last updated" timestamp at the bottom of your certification for the exact time.
